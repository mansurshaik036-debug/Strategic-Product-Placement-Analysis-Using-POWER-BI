# Executive Summary -- Strategic Product Placement Analysis

**Date**: 3 July 2026  
**Dataset**: 1,000 product-placement observations  
**Total Sales**: Rs.50,007,530 | **Estimated Profit**: Rs.4,343,015 | **Margin**: 8.7%

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Placement Drives Performance
End-cap placements generate the highest sales at Rs.17.42M (34.8% of total), outperforming Aisle by 3.4% and Front of Store by 10.3%. Product location is a material driver of revenue.

### 2. Clothing Leads Categories
Clothing is the strongest category with Rs.17.31M in sales (34.6% share), followed closely by Food (Rs.16.36M) and Electronics (Rs.16.34M). The narrow gap suggests Electronics has recovery potential.

### 3. Promotions Underperform
Promoted products generated Rs.24.55M vs Rs.25.46M for non-promoted -- a negative lift of Rs.0.91M. Promotions are not driving incremental revenue and may be eroding margin.

### 4. College Students Are the Core Demographic
College students drive Rs.12.86M (25.7%) of total sales, making them the highest-value segment. Targeted campus campaigns could unlock further growth.

### 5. Two Products Require Immediate Attention
- **Product 115581**: Top performer (Rs.241K sales, Rs.17.7K profit) -- protect and scale
- **Product 176976**: High sales (Rs.198K) but 0% margin -- investigate repricing or supplier terms

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Prioritize End-cap** for strategic SKUs and new launches
2. **Expand Clothing inventory** across all placements
3. **Review Electronics** through price/placement/promotion tests
4. **Align premium products** with high-traffic locations
5. **Evaluate promotions** on margin, not just volume
6. **Protect Product 115581** with premium placement
7. **Investigate Product 176976** for repricing
8. **Target College students** with dedicated campaigns
9. **Analyze outliers** for best-practice patterns
10. **Improve data collection** with date, geography, and cost fields
